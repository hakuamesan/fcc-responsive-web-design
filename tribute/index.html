How do you define Yoga?

Yoga, as described in the classical text 'Yoga Sutra' by Patanjali, is a way of calming the mental agitation so that you can let your true self emerge.

This can be achieved in 8 easy steps, namely:

Step 1: Yam

The first step, yama, deals with one's ethical standards and sense of integrity, focusing on our behavior and how we conduct ourselves in life. There are 5 yama's - Ahimsa (non-violence), Satya (truthfulness), Asteya (non-stealing), Brahmacharya (not sexual), Aparigraha (non-covetousness).

Step 2: Niyam

The second step has to do with self-discipline and spiritual observances. There are 5 niyama's - Saucha (cleanliness), Santosha (happiness/contend), Tapas (intensity/passion), Svadhyaya (Self-study), Ishvara pranidhana (surrender to God).

Step 3: Asana

This is the most popular step that almost everyone knows about and learns in typical Yoga classes.

Step 4: Pranayama

Prana (life force) and yama (restriction) brings about a control over your mind by restricting or controlling your breathing. The ancient yogi's found a direct relation between your breathing and your state of mind.

Step 5: Pratyahara

Withdrawal of your senses from the external stimuli.

Step 6: Dharana

Further refinements to the previous steps of Pranayama and Pratyahara by controlling the distractions to your mind.

Step 7: Dhyana

Also known as Meditation.

Step 8: Samadhi

Enlightenment.

Way Forward

Keep calm and do Yoga!
